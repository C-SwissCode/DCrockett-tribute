Davey Crockett

The Great Revolutionary Texas Fighter

Davey Crockett Fights at The Alamo.

Here lies the first words about Davey Crockett; the Texan with an attitude. There will be many tales to tell here, some may call them tall tales but I assure you these are true facts written in Texas history books. The private and home school history books tell it like it is. He started out in Tennessee, but he made darn sure to finish strong in Texas. In the end he fought with all he had leaving his guts out at the Alamo on that fateful night in San Antonio. This is a Tribute to Davey Crockett, The Republic of Texas, and the Alamo!

Davey's 3 Stages of Life That Led to His Legendary Status:

Before Texas

Crockett started his young life out in eastern parts of Tennessee where he grew into a legendary hunter. He made 2 stints in the US Congress first being elected in 1827 then losing for the final time in 1835. He grew bitter from that final defeat which led him to storm off to Texas to find some nice free land.

Journey To Texas

He likely traveled through the forbidden land of Arkansas to get to Texas (currently occupied by the Mexicans as the Mexican State of Tejas). Being the wild man that he was, he decided to take up arms against the Mexican army and helped lead the fight in the Texas Revolution.

The Alamo and The End

Refusing to give up in the war against the Mexican army, he would stay and fight a suicide mission at the Alamo Mission in San Antonio, where he and his men were slaughtered by the tyrant Santa Anna. His bravery and the men he fought with will never be forgotten, as it was there spirit and fight that led to a free country, The Republic of Texas!
